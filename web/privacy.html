Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 26, 2026

Hey804 is a civic information service built for the Hack for RVA 2026 hackathon. This policy describes what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

What We Collect

  • Phone number — when you text the Hey804 number, your phone number is stored to deliver replies.
  • Message content — the text of your questions is logged to improve the service. Messages containing personally identifiable information (SSNs, credit card numbers) are automatically redacted before storage.
  • Language preference — detected from your messages (English or Spanish).
  • Web usage — basic request data (IP address, timestamp) for the web app. No cookies or tracking scripts are used.

What We Do NOT Collect

  • Names, addresses, or account numbers
  • Social Security numbers (detected and redacted)
  • Location data (unless you scan a location-specific QR code, which only tells us which poster you scanned)

How We Use Your Data

  • To reply to your questions with verified city services information
  • To understand which topics residents ask about most, so we can improve the service

Data Sharing

We do not sell, share, or provide your data to third parties. Message delivery is handled by Twilio, which processes phone numbers and message content as a service provider under their own privacy policy.

Data Retention

  • Conversation logs: retained for 30 days, then deleted.
  • Subscriber records: retained until you opt out (text STOP), then hard-deleted.

Your Rights

  • Opt out anytime: text STOP to the Hey804 number. You will receive no further messages.
  • Request deletion: text STOP and your subscriber record will be removed.
  • Access your data: contact us at the email below to request a copy of data associated with your phone number.

Contact

This service was built for the Hack for RVA 2026 hackathon. For questions about this privacy policy, contact the Hey804 team through the hackathon organizers at rvahacks.org.
